**[Standardize Errors and Restrict Module Access]**
**Tangle:** The `GlossaError` was heavily duplicated in function signatures across different domains instead of a canonical type alias, leading to boilerplate. Furthermore, internal submodules within `morphology`, `parser`, `semantic` and `tools` were exposed as `pub mod` unnecessarily.
**Blueprint:** Refactored `.unwrap()` calls gracefully within the error handling where needed, applied a module-level generic `pub type Result<T, E = GlossaError> = std::result::Result<T, E>` in `errors.rs` instead of recursive aliases, unified its usage everywhere under `crate::errors::Result`. Additionally restricted submodules across the `src/` hierarchy to `pub(crate)` allowing strict compilation barriers.
